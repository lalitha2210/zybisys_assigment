Synopsis:

A thousand years ago, the original Thirteen Court Guard Squads consisted of ruthless and barbaric killers without shared purpose, who partook in slaughtering the Wandenreich. Yamamoto and Chōjirō defeated Yhwach; afterwards, Yamamoto adopted a peaceful philosophy and formed a unified Court Guard system. In the present, Yamamoto is bisected and disintegrated by Yhwach. Ichigo breaks through the Jail and finds a dying Byakuya, who tearfully asks Ichigo to protect the Soul Society before his Zanpakutō shatters. Ichigo trades blows with Yhwach's Reishi Sword using Getsuga Tenshō, but is quickly defeated. Yhwach attempts to stab Ichigo in the neck but is countered. Yhwach reveals Ichigo's mother was Quincy, making him part-Quincy, which allowed Ichigo to break Quilge's Jail and unconsciously counter Yhwach's attack. Shadows appear to transport the Quincy back to Silbern and Yhwach realizes that Aizen used his shikai on him to lose track of time. Ichigo tries to strike Yhwach again, but his Zanpakutō is destroyed by Jugram Haschwalth.

Description:

Burīchi: Sennen Kessen-hen) is a Japanese anime television series based on the Bleach manga series by Tite Kubo and a direct sequel to the Bleach anime series. In March 2020, Weekly Shōnen Jump and "Bleach 20th Anniversary Project & Tite Kubo New Project Presentation" livestream announced that the manga's last story arc, "Thousand-Year Blood War", would receive an anime project.[1] In November 2021, it was announced that the anime project would be a television series. The trailer and visual for the series were revealed at the Jump Festa '22 on December 18, 2021.[2][3] The series is directed by Tomohisa Taguchi and premiered on TV Tokyo on October 11, 2022.[4][5][6][a] The series will run for four cours with breaks in between.[6][7] The first cour is set to consist of 13 episodes.[8] Tatsuya Kitani [ja] performs the opening theme "Scar" (スカー, Sukā), while SennaRin performs the ending theme "Saihate" (最果て, "The Farthest Reaches");[9] In addition, Kitani also performed the special ending theme for the first episode, "Rapport", which was previously used as the main theme song for the series' 20th Anniversary Exhibition, Bleach EX.[10] On October 3, 2022, Viz Media announced that the series would stream on Hulu in the United States and Disney+ internationally outside of Asia.[11] Viz Media's English dub of the series began streaming on Hulu on November 4, 2022.[12]
